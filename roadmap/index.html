What's Next?

With v9.0.0 the canvas feature contract, hybrid CPU-GPU rendering, multi-magnifier workflow, and automated test suite are now in place. The focus shifts to evolving Improve-ImgSLI from a single-purpose image comparison utility into a full "media comparison factory" — with workspaces, video comparison, and a more complete editing pipeline.

Planned Features & Improvements

  • Workspaces & Tabs: The tab system has been tested and works, but a new tab type beyond the comparison view will not arrive before June or July 2026. The longer-term goal is full project management — handling multiple comparison sessions and complex workflows in parallel.
  • Video Comparison: Extending the comparison foundation from still images toward side-by-side video comparison. This depends on first finishing several pending improvements in the core image workflow.
  • Video Editor Completion: The keyframe-based timeline introduced in v8.1.0 still needs to grow into a proper editing workflow. The keyframe-read side of the render pipeline also needs revisiting — it works, but its overall pattern is not yet satisfying.
  • Filename & Text Overlays: The filename and text overlay system still needs more work to give users finer control over typography, positioning, and styling for exported comparisons.
  • Color Picker: A dedicated color picker is planned to make customization of magnifier colors, guide lines, separators, and overlay elements more direct and consistent.
  • Canvas Feature Contract Consolidation: The contract introduced in May 2026 is not perfect yet. The goal is that by version 10 or 11 the remaining feature logic will be consolidated enough that adding or removing a canvas feature no longer requires chasing hardcoded references across the project.
  • Multi-Magnifier Evolution: The multi-magnifier system shipped in v9.0.0 establishes the first usable foundation. Expect further refinement of interaction, highlighting, and the way multiple magnifiers behave under deletion, hiding, focus, export, zoom, guide lines, and comparison modes.
  • sli UI Toolkit as a Standalone Library: The reusable widget layer has been extracted into a package form named sli, but it is not fully standalone yet for a couple of technical reasons. Pushing it the rest of the way will let the toolkit be reused by other projects without dragging Improve-ImgSLI internals along.
